    BACKGROUND

    What is XAI? Explainable Artificial Intelligence or Interpretable Artificial Intelligence topic has taken special attention nowadays from academic researchers and professionals due to the significance of the XAI. DARPA explains XAI as build more explainable models while retaining high levels of learning success (prediction accuracy): and assist human users in comprehending, correctly trusting, and successfully managing the next wave of AI patterns. XAI is important for economic, commercial, and profitable welfare, for ethical issues or governing applications since the users have to understand, trust adequately, and handle AI outcomes effectively and properly. When it involves in such areas as healthcare, legal domain, banking, insurance, military, consultancy, etc, the users of these 2 areas often do not trust a machine learning model if they do not understand why, it made a given decision. There are several scenarios where there must be responsibility, accountability and transparency in the model which allowed the domain experts to make decisions without a doubt on the model. Regarding the situation of a physiologist who is a domain expert in deciding on a disease or medication, when they use AI models to simulate those because they are overly sensitive scenarios, and they have the highest degree of risk. Since of that a proper interpretation for the outcome of the AI model, there must be a possibility for improvement of the reliability and the transparency of the model. The Machine Learning model's trustworthiness is dependent on certain points, such as model accuracy and biased sampling of data. The accuracy of the model outcome is especially important for the correctness of the decision as well as the explanation but there may be some models which gives a better accuracy but, without explanation, the outcome makes a vast uncertainty to make the decision immediately. There are several cases in which machine learning software is biased and unjust decisions are arguably made and in those cases for a better model outcome the bias should be identified avoided and clearly identified the effect of an explanation for the Black-Box models. Regarding to the main two branches of Machine Learning Models, ‘White Box Models’ are the kind of models that can be clearly illustrated how they work, how predictions are made and what the influencing variables are. Due to the simple representation used, such models, as decision trees, linear regression, and Naïve Bayes, are intrinsically easier to understand. But regarding the ‘Black Box Models,’ it gives better accuracy than white-box models sometimes result in 1pc to 3pc, but it loses transparency and accountability. For example, Neural Networks, Support Vector Machines, Random Forests, K-Nearest Neighbors and Boosted are considered as Black Box Models and incomprehensible and behave like black boxes. A Survey on XAI gives four components that lead towards more trustworthy AI systems.

    • Explain to Justify.
    • Explain to Control.
    • Explain to Improve.
    • Explain to Discover.

    Well-fitted ML models with good predictive output often make decisions across complicated pathways because of the difficulty of these real-world problems. Human[1]comprehensible interpretations directly from the models are difficult to obtain. The lack of interpretability and transparency could lead to hidden prejudices and potentially dangerous behavior, which could impede the implementation of ML models in the real world. Best model explanation techniques are needed to address this challenge. Regarding to the Local Rule based Explanations, Fairness Measure, Interpretability of K-NN with high dimensional state and Providing unified explanation for CNN based predictions are approached in this MAIML Package.

    RESEACRH GAP

    The existing research studies which have investigated the curse of dimensionality are implemented with very difficult technologies and the results of the many approaches are also bias to the technical background and do not fulfil all the requirements which are met when addressing the high dimensionality problems. Since Shapley Additive Explanations are introduced very soon, it is very hard to find the studies where the SHAP values are used to this problem. Hence the curse of dimensionality problem is addressed by generating feature values and creating a dissimilarity measure by using SHAP values is needed to overcome the existing difficulties in high-dimensional K-NN. Many recent studies involved in the disease diagnosis using CXRs there is no clear involvement of explainable AI methods to describe the predictions. In single explanation method is used which is unique to the CXRs. According to the best knowledge so far, a collaborative approach to explain CXRs are not considered in previous studies. Hence a unified approach is needed to enhance the explainability. The proposed local rule-based explanation methods are mainly focusing on binary predictors. In, they have mentioned a future work to implement a method that can be compatible with multivalued classifications. The existing method has been used genetic algorithms to generate neighbourhoods for given input instances. In such a case, a distance method also can be used. In this research, both approaches will be compared and identified the most efficient and less complex method. For the multivalued classification, the SVM model does not natively support it. Therefore, to implement a solution for that, the multivalued classification needs to be broken down into multiple binary classifications where the SVM performs well EQO and ROBC are methods of finding fairness in ML models in the post-processing stage. Both methods are popular methods to address the fairness problem with significant research background. However, OQO and ROBC both are not yet developed as framework or libraries to use in practical development and testing environments. Implementing such methods on frequent basis to test ML models to detect fairness is inefficient process for the developers and ML practices. Therefor there is a lack of development tools to test models efficiently. The proposed local rule-based explanation methods are mainly focusing on binary predictors.
