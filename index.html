  • Welcome to MAIML: Model Agnostic Interpretable Machine Learning


    When the topic of Artificial Intelligence becomes more popular and the community is building up around it, transparency has been performing a significant role in that area. Decision-making systems that use AI for decision making, lack transparency or interpretability has become a major drawback when it comes to real-world critical scenarios. Nowadays, many countries have come up with rules and regulations to prohibit the use of black-box models where the model interpretability is hidden (e.g.: European GDPR). Therefore, the area of Model Interpretability (XAI) is being raised as a hot topic and it is the next step of AI. Even though the topic has become popular, the number of studies that have been done related to that area is less. In this study, the objective is to provide a framework by addressing this issue in four different aspects. The MAIML framework provides a fairness measure for Neural Networks, a Rule-based explanation for machine learning models based on SVM, an explanation for binary classification of K-NN algorithm in high dimensional state and a unified method for explaining CNNs using current techniques. The experiments for all four various fields are done and the results show the improvements of each field attached with the explainability/ interpretability.,
